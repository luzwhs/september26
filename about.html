About September 26th website

A coding class this might be interesting. I am sure this is what three students thought when they first started the class. Little did they know that the coding class would bring more realizations to them than just coding. Luz decided to sit towards the back of the room so she could sit and observe without too much interference. She sat right next to Klea and they had an instant connection. Sitting on the same side but all the way in front of the class was Connie. Nothing special so far as the women were concerned.

September 26, 2016 Luz informed the professor that she would be out as it was her birthday and her family was taking her out. She was surprised to find out that there were two other people who shared her birthday in the class. The next class, Luz being the curious Libra that she is, found out that Klea and Connie were her two fellow birthday sharers. The fates would intertwine again when numbers were randomly chosen for a group project. Klea, Connie, and Luz ended up choosing number three. It was then that the three Lovely Libra women knew exactly what their group project would be. Welcome to our September 26th website. We hope you will enjoy it as much as we enjoyed putting it together.

Constance Winston

Connie Winston has been a working actress in New York City for over 20 years, and has performed at New York City venues such as the WorkShop Theater, H.E.R.E., LaMaMa, E.T.C., New Dramatists, Ensemble Studio Theatre, and Soho Rep. Her current project, On Griffin Alley, is a re-creation of the trial and subsequent execution of Lena Baker, the only woman to be executed in the State of Georgia, and will be performed at Dixon Place in New York in March 2017.Connie has a M.F.A. in dramaturgy from the University of Iowa, a M.A. in performing arts from Emerson College, and a B.A. in theatre (with a concentration in acting and directing) from SUNY/New Paltz. Occasionally, Connie may be seen on re-runs of Law & Order and Conviction in her recurring role as Judge Shirley Taylor.

Luz Whetstone

I am a first generation Puerto Rican woman born and raised on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. I am the CEO/Founder of Mami's Angels llc., an organization that consults educators, and families on the importance of quality education for everyone. I have made a career of helping families, children, and communities learn how to empower themselves. I am a strong advocate and educator who is not afraid to take risks. My goal is to bring awareness to topics that might not always be given the awareness they need.
